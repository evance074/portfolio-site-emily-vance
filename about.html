About

My Story

I'm a UX designer with a background in human-centered design (HCD), behavior change, international development, and project management.

Before transitioning to UX, I worked in global health, using HCD to design and implement behavior change solutions. I facilitated workshops in Botswana, Mozambique, Malawi, and Tanzania, collaborating with stakeholders to develop interventions that addressed public health challenges.

At USAID Advancing Nutrition, I worked on the Social and Behavior Change (SBC) and Monitoring, Evaluation, and Learning (MEL) teams, where I designed workflows, managed operations, and co-authored evaluative guides. Through this work, I saw firsthand how thoughtful design and research shape impactful solutions.

Recognizing the need to build technical design skills, I pursued UX design to merge my expertise in research, strategy, and human-centered problem-solving.
